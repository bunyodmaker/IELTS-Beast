Passage 1 – Chocolate for the Masses

Chocolate, derived from the cacao bean, has been consumed for centuries. The Aztecs and Mayans valued it as a sacred drink. However, it was only in the 19th century that chocolate became affordable for ordinary people. The invention of the cocoa press in 1828 made it possible to separate cocoa butter from the bean, leading to the creation of solid chocolate. By the 1860s, companies like Cadbury and Nestlé began mass-producing chocolate bars. Today, chocolate is a multi-billion dollar industry.

Passage 2 – The Rise of Celebrity Culture

The concept of celebrity is not new, but its scale has exploded in the 21st century. Social media platforms like Instagram and TikTok have turned ordinary people into global stars overnight. This phenomenon raises questions about fame, privacy, and the impact on mental health. While some celebrities use their platform for positive change, others struggle with the constant pressure of public scrutiny. Researchers argue that this culture reflects society's deep need for connection and hero worship.

Passage 3 – Understanding Teaching Styles

Effective teaching is not one-size-fits-all. Educational research identifies three main styles: authoritarian (teacher-centered), permissive (student-led), and authoritative (balanced). The authoritative style, which combines high expectations with warmth, is linked to the best academic outcomes. In recent years, technology has introduced new dimensions, such as flipped classrooms and personalized learning. Teachers must adapt their style to the subject matter and the needs of their students.
